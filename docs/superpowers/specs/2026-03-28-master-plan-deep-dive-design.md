### Tiered Delay Strategy

```
August checkpoint
├── On track → November 2026 move (best case, before Jan 1)
├── Close but not quite → December 2026 move (still before Jan 1, no extra 住民税)
│   Adds: ~¥60,000 salary + ~¥150,000 side income = ~¥210,000 extra
│   New total: ~¥1,095,000
└── Not on track → January 2027 move (leave by Jan 31, accept small 住民税 hit)
    └── Still not ready by Jan → Serious reassessment needed
```

### January Delay: 住民税 Impact

If you miss the December 31 deadline, being registered in Japan on January 1, 2027 triggers 住民税 assessment on ALL 2027 income — even income earned in Malaysia after departure.

| Departure timing | 住民税 on 2027 income | Extra savings gained | Net gain |
|-----------------|----------------------|---------------------|----------|
| November 2026 | ¥0 | ¥0 (baseline) | Baseline |
| December 2026 | ¥0 | ~¥210,000 | **+¥210,000** |
| January 31, 2027 | ~¥30,000 (Jan salary only) | ~¥440,000 | **+¥410,000** |
| February 28, 2027 | ~¥80,000 (Jan-Feb salary) | ~¥670,000 | **+¥590,000** |
| March 2027+ | ~¥100,000–180,000+ | Diminishing returns | Clock is ticking |

**Key insight:** January departure is not terrible — the 住民税 hit (~¥30,000) is small relative to the extra savings (~¥440,000). But every month past January erodes the benefit. If you delay to January, **leave by January 31 at the latest.**

**The trap:** Once past Jan 1, there's a temptation to keep delaying ("just one more month of savings"). Each month adds income but also adds 住民税 liability. The net gain per month decreases. Set a hard deadline.

*Jan exit costs increased by ¥30,000 for 住民税 hit.*

**December is the realistic target.** It gives a small but positive landing buffer at mid-case estimates, preserves the January 1 住民税 advantage, and is only 1 month later than the original plan. November is possible only if side income exceeds conservative projections.

### Framing

**The real safety net is income, not savings.** Savings cover the transition. Ongoing income against ~$2,000/mo KL expenses is what makes this sustainable. The plan succeeds or fails on income velocity, not savings buffer. With ¥60,000/mo savings, side income isn't a nice-to-have — it's the difference between going and not going.

## Module 2: Japan Exit Procedures Playbook

### Purpose

A step-by-step playbook for cleanly exiting Japan — covering resignation, government procedures, financial transitions, and administrative closures. This is entirely absent from the current plan.

### Timeline: 2-3 Months Before Departure (Aug–Sep 2026)

#### Resignation (退職)

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Notice period | Check 就業規則 — most companies expect 1-3 months. Legal minimum is 2 weeks. |
| Timing | Give notice immediately after visa approval (mid-September). Target last working day: late October. |
| Coordinate both resignations | Ensure both final paychecks land before departure. Stagger if needed for different notice periods. |
| Submit 退職届 | Formal notification, not a request (退職願). |
| 有給休暇 strategy | Use all remaining paid leave at the end. Legally entitled. 10-15 days each = 2-3 weeks of paid time for move prep while still on shakai hoken. |
| Documents to collect | 退職証明書 (certificate of resignation), 源泉徴収票 (withholding tax statement) — needed for final tax return. |

#### 納税管理人 (Tax Representative)

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| What | Person in Japan authorized to receive tax notices and make payments on your behalf |
| Who | Family member (simplest) or tax accountant (税理士, ¥30,000–50,000) |
| How | Submit 納税管理人の届出書 to both: local tax office (税務署) AND city/ward office (区役所/市役所) |
| Deadline | Before departure. Recommend by October 15, 2026. |
| Why critical | Without this, 住民税 bills have nowhere to go and the city may pursue collection. This is load-bearing for the financial plan. |

### Timeline: 1 Month Before Departure (Oct 2026)

#### Apartment

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Give notice | Early October for end-of-October move-out (1 month notice). |
| 敷金 | Expect ¥0–50,000 return after cleaning deductions. Document condition with photos at move-out. |

#### Health Insurance Transition

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| 社会保険 ends | On last day of employment |
| Bridge coverage | If any gap between last employment day and departure, must enroll in 国民健康保険 at city hall. Mandatory. |
| Strategy | Use 有給休暇 to stay employed as close to departure as possible, minimizing the gap. |
| Cost | ~¥30,000–50,000/mo per person if enrolled. For a 1-week gap, prorated. |
| Cancellation | 国民健康保険 cancelled automatically when 転出届 is submitted. |

#### National Pension (国民年金)

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| After resignation | Automatically switched from 厚生年金 to 国民年金 Category 1. |
| Cost | ~¥16,980/mo per person |
| Budget | 1-2 months × 2 people = ¥34,000–68,000 |
| 脱退一時金 | Not claiming. 2-year decision window preserved. |

### Timeline: 2 Weeks Before Departure (mid-Oct 2026)

#### 転出届 (Moving-Out Notification)

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Where | City/ward office (区役所/市役所) |
| When | Up to 14 days before planned departure date |
| What it does | Removes you from 住民登録 (resident register). Officially makes you a non-resident of Japan. |
| Bring | Passport, My Number card, 印鑑 |
| Result | Receive 転出届証明書. Keep it. |
| My Number card | Returned/invalidated at this step. |

**January 1 Rule:** Because departure is before January 1, 2027, you will NOT be assessed 住民税 for 2027 income. This saves ¥400,000–800,000+. Do not delay past December 31 under any circumstances.

### After Departure (from Malaysia)

#### 確定申告 (Final Tax Return for 2026)

| Item | Detail |

## Module 3: Malaysia Setup & Tax Obligations

### Purpose

Expand the current plan's sparse "arrive and set up bank account" into a comprehensive first-month playbook, plus clarify tax obligations and business entity strategy.

### Week 1 in KL (November 2026)

| Action | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Immigration | Complete DE Rantau arrival formalities |
| SIM card | Hotlink or Digi at KLIA airport. ~MYR 30-50 for tourist SIM, convert to postpaid after getting bank account. Passport required. |
| Grab app | Primary transport. Set up immediately. |
| Touch 'n Go eWallet | Malaysia's dominant e-wallet. Load at convenience stores. Used for tolls, parking, many merchants. |

### Week 2-3: Bank Account

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Recommended | Maybank (largest network) or CIMB |
| Requirements | Passport, DE Rantau visa approval letter, proof of address |
| Challenge | Some branches want a utility bill for proof of address — catch-22 for newcomers. Airbnb confirmation may or may not be accepted. |
| Backup | GXBank (digital bank, easier for foreigners) or BigPay |
| Strategy | Try Maybank first with visa letter + Airbnb booking. If rejected, try another branch or CIMB. Budget 1-3 attempts. |
| Wise as bridge | Continue receiving USD income via Wise. Transfer to MY bank only for local expenses. Better exchange rates than banks. |

### Week 2-4: Housing Transition

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| First month | Airbnb in Mont Kiara. MYR 3,000-4,500/month. |
| Apartment search | iProperty.com.my, PropertyGuru, local agents. Visit in person — photos can mislead. |
| Typical lease terms | 1-year minimum. Upfront: 2 months deposit + 0.5 month utility deposit + 1 month advance = ~3.5x monthly rent. |
| For MYR 3,000/mo | ~MYR 10,500 (~$2,270) upfront |
| Negotiation | Some landlords accept 1+1 deposit for shorter initial terms. Worth asking. |

### Malaysia Tax Obligations

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Tax residency trigger | 182+ days in a calendar year |
| 2026 | ~60 days (Nov-Dec). NOT tax resident. No MY tax filing needed for 2026. |
| 2027 | Full year = tax resident if staying all year |
| What's taxed | Malaysian-sourced income + foreign-sourced income remitted to Malaysia (since 2022) |
| DE Rantau income | Foreign-sourced. Technically taxable when remitted to MY bank account. |
| Tax rates | Graduated: 0% on first MYR 5,000 up to 30% at top bracket. Effective rate at ~$4,000/mo income: ~8-14%. |
| Practical approach | Keep income in Wise/foreign accounts. Transfer to MY bank only what's needed for local expenses. Reduces taxable remittance. |
| Professional advice | Consult a Malaysian tax advisor specializing in expats before end of 2027. Budget MYR 500-1,000. |
| Filing deadline | April 30 of following year (April 30, 2028 for 2027) |

### Business Entity Strategy

| Stage | Entity | Reasoning |
|-------|--------|-----------|
| Pre-revenue / early | No entity (sole proprietor) | Zero cost, zero complexity. Receive payments via Stripe/Gumroad → Wise. |
| SaaS > $2,000/mo consistently | US LLC via Stripe Atlas | Clean separation of personal/business. ~$500 setup + $100+/yr registered agent. |
| Later (if needed) | Malaysian SDN BHD | Only if local presence required. MYR 1,000-3,000 setup, requires local director. Overkill for early stage. |

Do NOT set up a Japan 個人事業主 — you'll have submitted 転出届 and running a JP business as non-resident adds complexity for no benefit.

## Module 4: Feasibility Stress-Test & Adjustments

### Purpose

Honest assessment of the plan's assumptions with adjustments where projections are optimistic.

### Assumption 1: 20 hrs/week side work while full-time

| Assessment | Detail |
|------------|--------|
| Risk | Burnout over 7 months. 10 hrs/week each after full-time + commute is aggressive. |
| 副業 policy | Must verify both employers allow side work (check 就業規則). Discovery could complicate resignation. |
| Adjustment | Model side income at 70% for April-June, full projections from July onward. Impact: -¥27,000 total. Small. |
| Action | Add checklist item: verify 副業 policy at both employers before starting side work. |

### Assumption 2: DataAnnotation/Outlier income starts April

| Assessment | Detail |
|------------|--------|
| Risk | Approval takes 1-6 weeks. Task availability is unpredictable — some weeks zero hours available. |
| Adjustment | Apply to ALL annotation platforms simultaneously in April W1 (DA, Outlier, Appen, Telus, Scale AI, Surge AI). Whichever approves first becomes Layer 1. |
| Action | Move I3 (backup platform applications) from April 15 to April 1. |

### Assumption 3: Upwork at $25-50/hr within 2 months

| Assessment | Detail |
|------------|--------|
| Risk | New accounts with zero reviews must undercut market rates to win first projects. |
| Adjustment | Explicit ramp: first 5 jobs at $15-20/hr for reviews, then raise. Don't expect $50/hr for 3-4 months. |
| Edge to exploit | Target JP companies on Upwork — bilingual JP↔EN developers have less competition and command better rates even with new accounts. |

### Assumption 4: SaaS MVP in 4-6 weeks

| Assessment | Detail |
|------------|--------|
| Risk | 40-60 hours at 10 hrs/week is tight. Scope ("upload → translate → review → export") is 3-4 features. |
| Adjustment | Strict validation gate: B1 must pass before B2 starts. Validation = 5 potential users say "I'd pay" + 2 agree to beta test. If validation fails, pivot to digital products only. |
| Scope reduction | True MVP: upload strings → AI translate → download. No review UI in v1. |

### Assumption 5: Partner's income trajectory

| Assessment | Detail |
|------------|--------|
| Risk | Content writing, VA, and social media management all require skill building. One HubSpot course ≠ job-ready. |
| Realistic projection | Partner reaches $300-500/mo by October, not $600-1,000/mo. |
| Reframe | Partner's most valuable near-term contribution may be handling move logistics (research, scheduling, document prep) — unpaid but high-ROI. Build explicit skill milestones, not just "sign up on platforms." |
